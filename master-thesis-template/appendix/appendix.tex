\section{Benchmark Preset Definitions}
\label{app:presets}

This appendix documents the complete preset definitions used in the benchmark evidence reported in the thesis. Each preset specifies the workload family, payload configuration, concurrency level, target rate where applicable, failure injection parameters, and resource overlay tier. The four reported presets are: \emph{synthetic-clean} for bulk transfer, \emph{synthetic-continuous} for steady-rate pacing, \emph{synthetic-backpressure} for slow-consumer overload, and \emph{synthetic-recovery} for forced-restart resilience. The harness also contains a CSV replay mode, but those results are not used in the current thesis argument.

\begin{table}[!htbp]
    \centering
    \scriptsize
    \caption{Benchmark presets used in the current transport study.}
    \label{tab:appendix-presets}
    \setlength{\tabcolsep}{3pt}
    \resizebox{\textwidth}{!}{%
    \begin{tabular}{lp{2.7cm}p{3.9cm}}
        \toprule
        Preset & Key parameters & Purpose \\
        \midrule
        \texttt{synthetic-clean} & Synthetic payloads from 256 B to 16 KiB; concurrency 1--16; ten repetitions; no injected failure & Baseline bulk-transfer throughput, latency, and correctness matrix \\
        \texttt{synthetic-continuous} & 1 KiB payloads, 1{,}000 msg/s target, concurrency 4 and 8, ten repetitions & Steady-rate pacing and jitter comparison \\
        \texttt{synthetic-backpressure} & 1 KiB payloads, 4{,}000 msg/s target, concurrency 8, 2 ms sink delay, ten repetitions & Slow-consumer overload and queueing behaviour \\
        \texttt{synthetic-recovery} & 4 KiB payloads, 2{,}000 msg/s target, concurrency 8, transformer restart after 3 s, 120 retries at 250 ms backoff, ten repetitions & Restart recovery, replay, and continuity analysis \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}}
\end{table}

        \midrule
        Default gRPC message ceiling & Original classic unary & Original DYNAMOS documentation reports a practical limit around 4 MB, or roughly 30k rows in that setup & Increased benchmark message limits for the baseline so the comparison measures buffering cost rather than an artificial ceiling & Chunking removes the dependency on one very large response message \\
        Full-result buffering & Classic unary and early bulk prototypes & Large results were materialised repeatedly before the client saw progress & Preserved 5k-row chunks through SQL, aggregate, algorithm, agent, and gateway stages & Supported by lower memory peaks and earlier partial results \\
        Stale generated jobs & Repeated classic unary runs & Later repetitions could return fast 502 or empty-provider responses when old generated jobs remained & Cleaned generated \texttt{surf}, \texttt{uva}, and \texttt{vu} jobs outside the measured request window & Not a streaming advantage; handled for benchmark fairness \\
        Multi-provider merge error & Classic multi-provider baseline & The first provider could be appended twice during aggregation & Applied a minimal aggregation correction before reporting multi-provider baseline timings & Baseline correctness fix, not a streaming feature \\
        Timeout and empty-provider reporting & Early large-result runs & Some failures appeared as missing final results or empty provider bodies & Made timeout/error outcomes visible in benchmark output and separated clean errors from valid completions & Improves diagnosability; not a full crash-recovery proof \\
